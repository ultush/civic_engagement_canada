Civic_Engagement_Canada wiki!

This project is an investigation into different aspects of civic engagement in Canada.

The short term purpose is to determine linkages between voter turnout and social sector organization density.

The medium term purpose is to determine the linkages between different aspects of civic engagement.

The long-term purpose to determine pathways for increasing civic engagement.

Scope: The current scope is the Greater Toronto Area, which provides some useful comparisons within the city of Toronto as well as comparisons with surrounding cities

The analysis can be replicated fr other metropolitan areas in Canada for broader comparisons

The roadmap and plan as it evolves:

References and Further Reading

The main study of interest that could be replicated in Canada:

  1. Kim, M. (2013). Socioeconomic Diversity, Political Engagement, and the Density of Nonprofit Organizations in US Counties. The American Review of Public Administration, 45(4), 402-416

Main points:

Higher Political Engagement (voter turnout in Presidential election) = Higher density of nonprofits in a US county

Higher Income Inequality (Gini Coefficient) = Higher density of nonprofits in a US county

Higher Ethno-Racial Diversity = Lower density of nonprofits in a US county

With ethno-racial diversity increasing in Canadian cities, the last conclusion is a cause for concern. With income inequality increasing in Canada, the second conclusion is positive for the nonprofit sector, but not necessarily positive for everyone.
